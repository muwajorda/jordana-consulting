🌟 About Me

I'm Jordana — a bioinformatics and computational biology expert with 6+ years of experience working across Uganda and the U.S. I’ve contributed to cancer research, drug discovery, and biomarker identification at institutions like Precede Biosciences, Foghorn Therapeutics, and the University of Alabama at Birmingham.

I'm currently an Independent Consultant & Researcher (Feb 2023 – Present), developing cloud-based AI models for early cancer detection and personalized treatment strategies. I utilize Google Cloud and TCGA datasets to analyze healthcare disparities in Black patients and am working towards the Google Cloud Associate Engineer Certification.

Previously, I was a Part-Time Lecturer at Northeastern University (Fall 2021 – May 2023), teaching “Scientific Information Management for Biotechnology Managers.” I covered data sciences, cloud computing, and modern biotech tools to equip future biotech leaders.

At Precede Biosciences (May 2022 – Feb 2023), I led NGS data analysis and AI-driven cancer prediction models. I developed pipelines for multi-omics biomarker discovery, analyzing Cell-Free ChIP-seq and MeDIP data to confirm cancer signals and build diagnostic classifiers.

At Foghorn Therapeutics (Feb 2019 – May 2022), I designed machine learning models to predict drug sensitivity in cancer and automated workflows for NGS and chromatin profiling using AWS and GCP. My work supported biomarker discovery and drug response analysis.

My bioinformatics journey started at the University of Alabama at Birmingham (Oct 2017 – Feb 2019), where I led an RNA-seq project investigating sex-based differences in early Parkinson’s disease. I identified gene expression biomarkers and potential therapeutic targets.

Outside of work, I enjoy traveling, walking, discovering new experiences, reading, and hanging out in cozy coffee shops ☕️. I’m passionate about building scalable, accessible solutions in precision medicine — especially tools for early diagnosis and equity in Africa and beyond.
